## Problem Statement

Event organizers (university clubs, community groups) waste 10+ hours/month on fragmented tools: KakaoTalk for announcements, Google Forms for registration, Excel for member management, manual bank transfers for deposits. No single tool handles the full workflow. Data dies after every event.

## Demand Evidence

- Direct observation: KUBA (Korea University Business Association) exchange student club operates 45th generation with 100+ members, managing everything manually
- Status quo cost: organizers use 5+ separate tools, spend estimated 480만원/month equivalent in time waste
- Working MVP deployed: TestFlight live, Railway backend running, real users testing
- Industry data: global event management market $22T, average no-show rate 25%

## Status Quo

Organizers currently:
1. KakaoTalk group chat for announcements (no structure, messages get buried)
2. Google Forms or manual headcount for registration (no payment integration)
3. Excel spreadsheets for member tracking (version conflicts, no real-time)
4. Bank transfer screenshots for payment verification (manual, error-prone)
5. No post-event data retention (every event starts from zero)

No dedicated app. Just manual labor and duct tape.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Primary user:** University club president/operations lead managing 50-200 members across 10+ events per semester.

**Narrowest wedge:** One-tap event registration with integrated payment and QR check-in. Replace the 5-tool workflow with one app. This alone saves 10+ hours/month and reduces no-show from 25% to target 8%.

## Premises

1. Event organizers will switch from free tools (KakaoTalk + Google Forms) if the time savings are dramatic enough (480만원 -> 80만원 equivalent)
2. University clubs are the right beachhead because they have recurring events, captive audiences, and low switching costs
3. BLE networking and blockchain attendance proof are Phase 2 differentiators that justify higher valuation, but Phase 1 SaaS must stand alone
4. 5-person team + AI can ship faster than a 20-person team without AI
5. Korean university market is the right starting point before Japan/Thailand expansion
